EUROPEAN COMMUNITIES ­ EXPORT SUBSIDIES ON SUGAR
Request to Join Consultations
Communication from Zimbabwe
The following communication, dated 9 October 2002, from the Permanent Mission of Zimbabwe to the Permanent Delegation of the European Commission, the Permanent Mission of Brazil and to the Chairman of the Dispute Settlement Body, is circulated in accordance with Article 4.11 of the DSU.
_______________
I make reference to the Communications from the Permanent Missions of Australia and Brazil addressed to the Permanent Delegation of the European Commission and to the Chairman of the Dispute Settlement Body entitled "European Communities - Export Subsidies on Sugar" and which were circulated on 1 October 2002, in accordance with Article 4.4 of the DSU, as Documents WT/DS265/1, G/L/569, G/AG/GEN/52 and G/SCM/D47/1 and WT/DS266/1, G/L/570, G/AG/GEN/53 and G/SCM/D48/1, respectively.
The Communications refer to consultation being sought with the European Communities (EC) pursuant to Article 4 of the Understanding on Rules and Procedures Governing the Settlement of Disputes (DSU), Article XXII:1 of the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade 1994 (GATT 1994), Article 19 of the Agreement on Agriculture, and Articles 4 and 30 of the Agreement on Subsidies and Countervailing Measures (SCM Agreement) regarding the EC's Common Organisation of the Market in sugar and its application and implementation, and to the export subsidies provided by the EC to the sugar sector, respectively.
In view of Zimbabwe's substantial trade interest in this matter as a supplier of sugar to the EC market, Zimbabwe is interested to join in the consultations between the EC, Australia and Brazil pursuant to Article 4.11 of the DSU.
