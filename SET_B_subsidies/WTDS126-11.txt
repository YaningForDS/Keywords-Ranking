AUSTRALIA ­ SUBSIDIES PROVIDED TO PRODUCERS AND EXPORTERS OF AUTOMOTIVE LEATHER
Notification of Mutually Agreed Solution
The following communication, dated 24 July 2000, from the Permanent Mission of the United States and the Permanent Mission of Australia to the Chairman of the Dispute Settlement Body, is circulated pursuant to Article 3.6 of the DSU.
_______________
The Governments of the United States and Australia wish to notify the Dispute Settlement Body (DSB) that they have reached a mutually satisfactory solution in regard to implementation of the findings in the Panel Report "Australia ­ Subsidies Provided to Producers and Exporters of Automotive Leather" (WT/DS126), Recourse to Article 21.5 of the DSU by the United States, adopted by the DSB on 11 February 2000.
The mutually satisfactory solution involves:
- payment of $A 7.2 million to the Australian Government by the company found to have been receiving assistance provided in a manner inconsistent with Australia's WTO obligations;
- removal of automotive leather from eligibility for support under certain Australian industry assistance programmes available to the textiles, clothing and footwear sector and the automotive sector, and a prohibition on other subsidies, either direct or indirect, that are related to or benefit the manufacture, sale or distribution of automotive leather for a period of 12 years;
- suspension of certain custom duties on a most favoured nation basis, with effect from 1 July 20001;
- binding arbitration in the event of a dispute over the implementation or application of the mutually satisfactory solution.

1 The tariff lines eligible for a suspension of custom duties are listed in the attachment to this notification.
